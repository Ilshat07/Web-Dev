  • Tokyo

    • Where can i go?

    1. Tokyo Imperial Palace

      2. The Tokyo Imperial Palace is the palace of the Emperor of Japan in the Chiyoda Special District of the Tokyo metropolitan area. Located on the grounds of the former Edo Castle. It has been used since the second half of the 19th century as the residence of emperors and the Imperial Court. It is under the control of the Office of the Imperial Household of Japan. The total area, including gardens, is 7.41 km². The architecture of the buildings of the complex is mixed: some buildings are built in European and others in traditional styles.

    2. Tokyo Skytree

      3. Tokyo Skytree is a television tower in the Sumida district of Tokyo, Japan, the tallest among the world's television towers and the third tallest building in the world after the Burj Khalifa and Merdeka 118. The height of the TV tower together with the antenna is 634 meters, which is almost twice as high as the Tokyo TV tower; the height of the tower was chosen so that the numbers: 6, 3, 4 were consonant with "Musashi" - the name of the historical area where modern Tokyo is located.

  • New-York

    • Where can i go?

    1. Central park

      2. Central Park in New York is one of the largest in the United States and the most famous in the world. The park is located on Manhattan Island between 59th and 110th Streets and Fifth and Eighth Avenues and has a rectangular shape. The length of the park is about 4 kilometers, the width is about 800 meters, the total area is 3.41 km².

    2. Statue of Liberty

      3. The Statue of Liberty is a colossal sculpture in the style of Napoleon III, or the style of the Second Empire, located in the United States on Liberty Island, located in Upper New York Bay about 3 kilometers southwest of the southern tip of Manhattan Island. It was built as a gift from France to the United States for the 1876 World's Fair and the centenary of American independence.
